### Financial Performance (Fiscal Year 2025)

While internal estimates indicated an annual revenue of $604,000,000 (approximately ₹5,013 crore), more recent external research provides detailed figures for Fiscal Year 2025 (FY25), reflecting significant growth.

*   **Total Operating Revenue (FY25)**: PhonePe's operating revenue soared to **₹7,631 crore** in FY25, marking an impressive **89% increase** compared to the previous financial year.
*   **Loss Reduction**: The company demonstrated disciplined scaling, with losses dropping by **13%** in FY25. Notably, excluding share-based payments to employees (₹2,358.6 crore), PhonePe would have reported a net profit of ₹638.4 crore in FY25.
*   **Revenue Breakdown by Segment (FY25)**:
    *   **Core Payments Business**: Contributed 88.5% of total operating revenue, reaching **₹6,299.7 crore**. This represents a **32% jump** from ₹4,788.5 crore in FY24.
    *   **Insurance and Lending Distribution Services**: This segment saw substantial growth, zooming nearly **208%** to **₹557.6 crore** in FY25 from ₹181.1 crore in the previous year. While rapidly growing, these services still constitute a smaller portion, contributing 8% to the company’s operating revenue in FY25.

*   **Key Cost Drivers (FY25)**:
    *   **Total Expenses**: Surged over 21% to ₹9,394.1 crore, up from ₹7,754.3 crore in the preceding financial year.
    *   **Employee Benefit Expense**: The largest cost driver, increasing 14% to **₹4,096.7 crore** in FY25 from ₹3,603.9 crore in FY24.
    *   **Payment Processing Charges**: Another major cost center, rising 45% to **₹1,688.2 crore** in FY25 from ₹1,166.4 crore in the previous fiscal year.

### Strategic Outlook

PhonePe is actively diversifying beyond its core payments business into higher-margin financial services such as lending and insurance. This strategy is crucial for achieving sustained profitability as it prepares for a targeted **$1.5 billion IPO in 2026**. The company aims to leverage its massive user base to drive these new ventures, navigating competitive landscapes and potential UPI market share regulations.
